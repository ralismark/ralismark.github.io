Who needs rules and mechanics when you can just house-rule everything and fudge all the outcomes?

> One of the participants is a narrator, the others play characters interacting with the narrator's world.
>
> When encountering an outcome that may in doubt, roll 1 or more dice.
> The narrator decides what happens based on how they feel about the result.
>
> Optionally, you may use any other system of random chance (e.g. deck of tarot/playing cards, pulling objects out of a bag, astrology) instead of dice.

That's the entire TTRPG system!

	This is spiritually an April Fools idea, but the more I think about it the more it seems not incredibly awful.
