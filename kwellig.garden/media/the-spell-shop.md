I found this book off a Reddit recommendation for books like _[The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet](./the-long-way-to-a-small-angry-planet)_, and yeah.
In a word, cozy 🥰

I love the main thesis of: community, what's essentially found family, and people looking out for each other just because they can.
It's so good !

The first half of the book was definitely slow.
It takes its time to settle in and get all the narrative threads in a row before it kicks off main complication -- almost 2/3 of the way through the book...
But once it did, the plot just kept tumbling on and on with new plot hooks all the way to the end.
It was a fun ride :)

I love how the folks are so ready to tell lies and rally behind each other to defend each other from the people from the capital.
I love the whole plausible deniability dance of Captain Varrick, it was really fun to see.

Larran.
I'm still kinda on the fence about him.
He's fine, his romance with the protagonist is fine, Kiela being like "what is this feeling" is pretty funny.
But I wasn't really excited for it...
Maybe it's kinda just a straight relationship lmao
(THOUGH THERE IS QUEER SHIT GOING ON IN THE BACKGROUND THAT I AM CHEERING FOR)

BUT.
HIS FAMILY.
OH MY GODS.
Immediate grasp on my heartstrings I'm sorry -- tldr: guy has been through so much loss.
Sounds like his father was verbally abusive, he killed his mum, then he died as well, leaving Larran an orphan.
Really brought a lot more depth to him, and similarly with Bryn and her backstory.

On the other hand, Fenerer was just ughhhhhh... thorn in your side.
Nothing really gets done about him and he gets unceremoniously shipped off the island at the end.
There's not much depth to him or character development, and I really wish there was some, because he's written too flatly, and it leaves the story just kinda hopeful instead of really pragmatic about actually uplifting _everyone_.
