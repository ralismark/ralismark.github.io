Anyways, I'm gonna bring that gas giant + habitable moon idea into Soupworld!
The main world is a moon called Soup, which is tidally locked around a green gas giant called Erin.
Inspired, of course, by Adrian[^adrian] (_Project Hail Mary_) and Jool (_Kerbal Space Program_).

# Erin, The Green Eye In The Sky

There's a _lot_ of fun implications of having such a large body always in the same place in the sky -- for one it'd be quite a handy navigational aid!
It turns out to be about the width of your hand at arm's length, about 10x to 20x the visual size of the moon.

.. figure:: {{ recipe.copy_ca("./erin.png") }}
	:alt: Wide-angle photo of the Sydney harbour skyline at night. Next to the tiny bit bright moon is an edited-in green sphere, about as tall as the towers in the city.

	Erin is so much bigger than the moon

Being so large, Erin reflects a lot of sunlight.
It's be over 300x the brightness of the moon here on Earth, lighting up the world at night with a green glow about as bright as streetlamps.
It also noticeably changes across the regular waxing and waning of Erin each night.
Depending on where you are, full Erin would occur at a different time of night -- ranging from sunrise at the retrograde point, to midnight at the radial point closest to Erin, to sunset at the prograde point (where "it's darkest before the dawn" is actually true!)

There's also daily eclipses from it occluding the sun that last around 1/50th of a day (~30mins), plunging its shadow into a darkness darker than even night.
It's the only time when Erin and the sun consent for the world to glimpse the starry vastness beyond.

Of course, _none_ of this is present on the farside.
There'll be the ordinary day-night cycle you'd get on earth, you wouldn't even have a moon.

I haven't even gotten to the biological and ecological and cultural implications of half the world having such a weird day-night cycle!

- I have no clue how it'd affect circadian rhythms, but places that get sufficiently lit at night might have a more developed nightlife.

- Street lighting might be less uncommon in the nearside, except in streets and alleyways that obscure Erin.
	In fact, main streets would point towards Erin to keep them naturally lit, but also as a cultural thing.

- The midday night would be a midday break time, where it's considered unwise to keep working... unless you're an astronomer!

There's so much more that could be explored...

# End

I find that a lot of fantasy settings in TTRPGs have a very pre-astronomy mythological view of stars, in which their movements foretell worldly events... ["As above, so below"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/As_above%2C_so_below), as this mechanism seems to be called.
Surprisingly few call out the wandering stars as _planets_, fewer have anything interesting going on in the sky.

Where are all the worlds with multiple moons, multiple stars, rings, or bodies in [trojan] or [horseshoe] orbits?
Where are the colourful nebulae and the visible light pulsars that blink like a lighthouse in the sky?
Where are the fantastical astronomical phenomenon?

[trojan]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trojan_(celestial_body)
[horseshoe]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horseshoe_orbit

Soupworld is my own, and so I will drip sci-fi into this fantasy world as I please.

# Appendix: How green?

I went on a bit of a rabbit hole trying to figure out how it could be physically possible, since it turns out green is _really_ hard to get in astronomy.

.. admonition:: me/warn

	**This is non-canon**, in Soupworld Erin's colour is handwaved away, since I'm not trying to do hard sci-fi any one bit.

	But I did go down the rabbit hole and came out with something cool, so I didn't want it to go to waste.
	It was going to get stuff into a footnote, but this was so long it broke my footnote processing!

First things first, [Sudarsky's Gas Giant Classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudarsky's_gas_giant_classification) suggests a few different classes of gas giants and what colour they'd appear.
None are green.
Chlorine would produce the perfect greenish-yellow colour, but apparently it'd be difficult to explain why there's so much chlorine, and it wouldn't be dominant in the clouds that give gas giants their colour either.
Oxygen radicals are an apple green (that's where aurorae get their colour!) but there's basically no way to have that either.
Another option seems to be an abundance of microorganisms, which is actually not too terrible to handwave into existence.

(To cite my sources, [this reddit post trying to justify KSP's Jool](https://www.reddit.com/r/KerbalSpaceProgram/comments/q2chuk/im_driving_myself_crazy_trying_to_scientifically/) goes through a lot of the possibilities)

The other direction you solve this is to modify perception.
Just as red-green colourblindness can arise from mutations affecting what wavelengths your cones are sensitive to, we might be able to make the blue or brown of gas giants register as green.
This has implications for what colour _everything else_ appears as.
In particularly, the green of plants.

Fortunately, we are helped by a whole 'nother rabbit hole I went down in earlier worldbuilding phase, as part of a separate "Project 3KK" (tldr: plants huddling for light under a dim 3000K brown dwarf/star).
Notably, I found a [reference chart of chlorophyll alternatives](https://www.reddit.com/r/worldbuilding/comments/1dui0v/plants_on_other_worlds_reference_chart/), which easily answers the question of how to finagle all the colour right!

Have a Class II gas giant, which is blue, and use Phycocyanin instead of Chlorophyll, which is also blue.
Shift green perception up into the blue range and tada!
As above (green of Erin), so below (green of the plants).
