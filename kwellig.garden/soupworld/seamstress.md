title: The Seamstress
date: 2026-03-24

In times long past was the Seamstress Of Woven Fates, who played the kith and kin of the world, knitting the strands of fate into a wondrous tapestry of myths and legends, of great triumphs and tragedies.
Many of our stories today are said to come from Her!
These tales She weaved pulled souls down the paths She imagined, leaving no slack for them to follow the yearning of their hearts.
And so in Her wake, there were many tears and curses sent Her way.

The Seamstress heard these cries and felt these tears, and they clung to Her heart until one day She could ignore it no more.
So, She loosened Her grips on the strings of the world, letting folks' stories unfold unbound.
And thus sprouted a web of connections between people spanning the world, much more complex and, dare I say messy, than anything She could ever conceive of even in an eternity.
She too was freed from always holding the reins of their stories!
Her precious time and imagination could be much better spent on unpicking knots and guiding the winds of fate towards serendipity.

Seeing this, She next invited the world to partake in Her storytelling, teaching them the songs of creation.
And so the world sang and danced in chorus with Her!
The birds developed their myriad calls, the folks their myriad words, and everywhere created their own stories to teach and to entertain and to remember.
An occasionally, even to change the flow of another's fate.

She could not help but marvel, and knew that Her title and role in picking at the threads of fate was no longer needed.
So as one final act, She fragmented Her title into the stars of the night and painted them across the sky.
A canvas for the world to span with constellations so that, just as She once did with the world, the world could draw upon Her image.
And monument to what She gave the world -- the freedom, hope, and agency to rewrite their own narratives!

.. details:: Author's Notes

	One day, I was visited by a bout of anxiety at midnight.
	So, in an attempt to stave it off, I turned my churning thoughts towards Soupworld, and weaved up this tale of the stars.

	The stars are a very significant and _innate_ part of the world.
	They are an explicitly magical source of power, able to bend the wills of the world.
	Towards this, they also symbolise the vastness of possibility, hope, and, tangentially, guidance.

	The constellations, meanwhile, are purely in the minds of the people.
	They are not universal.
	Different folks memorialise different ideas through constellations.

	Also mentioned herein is the "Songs of Creation".
	There is no lore behind it as of yet, but it's a hook into future work!

	I was very sad I couldn't slip a "dis-aster" pun into this.
	However, I did manage "draw upon Her image", with the double meaning of both coming up with constellations and wielding Her magic.
